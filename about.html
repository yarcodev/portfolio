About my journey

About Me

Hello! My name is Yar Zar Min Htike and my username is "yarcodev". I am from Myanmar and graduated with a degree in Mathematics.

This website is more than just a portfolio—it's a place where I document my learning journey, share what I build, record each step, celebrate small milestones, and look back on how far I've come.

Before Touching Code

Honestly, I didn't even know what programming meant before I started learning to code. During the COVID-19 pandemic in 2021 and 2022, along with the difficult situation in my country, I spent most of my time at home helping my family.

During that time, I started thinking more seriously about my future. I had graduated in Mathematics, but I realized I had forgotten a lot of what I had learned. So I went back to my old notes and started studying again.

While searching for learning resources, I found Khan Academy to review Mathematics and learn more. One evening, while I was exploring Khan Academy, I became curious about what else they taught. That's when I accidentally discovered the Computer Science course.

I had a few friends who studied Computer Science at university, but I had never asked them what they actually studied. I assumed it was mostly about Microsoft Word, Excel, or fixing computers, because that was all I knew about computers at the time. Looking back, I realize how little I understood about the field. During my university years, I was simply enjoying life with my friends and had never imagined that software development would one day become my passion.

The Beginning

Before writing my first line of code, I spent time learning the fundamentals in the Computer and Internet section on Khan Academy. At that time, I didn't even own a computer or a MacBook to practice programming, so I focused on understanding how computers actually work.

That course helped me understand how computers work, how they store information, and how they process data. What interested me the most was how closely it connected with mathematics, like binary numbers, decimal numbers, hexadecimal numbers, bits, bytes, and logic. Learning those topics made me even more excited to start programming.

Diving Into Code

When I first chose a course on Khan Academy, I didn't really understand the differences between programming languages or what each one was used for. I simply chose JavaScript because it was the only programming language I had heard of before.

Every time I started a new lesson, I became even more excited. I was always curious about what I would learn next, and I never skipped the coding challenges because I wanted to solve every one of them by myself.

After finishing the JavaScript course, I moved on to HTML and CSS. Once again, I got excited about learning something new. This time, I wanted to understand how websites actually work. One thing that fascinated me the most was how links connected pages together and allowed people to navigate through a website. That curiosity gave me the confidence to start building my own websites.

At the time, I didn't know whether I was choosing the right course or not. I simply followed my curiosity. Looking back now, I think that was one of the best decisions I made.

Starting with the Computer and Internet course gave me a solid foundation. Learning JavaScript next felt natural because its logic and problem-solving reminded me of mathematics. After that, moving on to HTML and CSS helped me understand how those programming concepts could be used to build real websites. Without realizing it, I had created a learning path that suited me perfectly.

Here is my first animation project and this is my first game that I built on Khan Academy. Looking back at those projects reminds me how every small step helped me grow. I'm grateful to Khan Academy for giving me such a strong foundation. I've learned a lot over the years.

Coding My Future

Today, while writing this page, I'm focused on learning DOM manipulation, responsive web design, and building real projects. Alongside this portfolio, I'm also creating my coffee website, allAboutCoffee.

I'm learning to code while working full-time as a barista. Programming isn't my full-time job, at least not yet. Every day, I try to balance work, learning, and building projects. Through this website, I hope to share what I learn while continuing to improve my own skills.

My long-term goal is to work in robotics and artificial intelligence. I've been fascinated by machines since I was young. Before reaching that goal, I want to begin my career as a frontend developer and build a strong foundation in software development.

Sometimes I worry that, because I'm self-taught, I might miss important concepts or take a longer path than others. But every challenge teaches me something new, and every project gives me more confidence. As long as I keep learning, I believe I'll eventually reach my goal.

Right now, my motivation to learn is stronger than ever.
I still have a lot to learn. Every project is another step in my journey.
I can't stop coding. The journey has just begun.
